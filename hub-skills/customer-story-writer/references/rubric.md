# Scoring Rubric

Manish's 3-point system for evaluating Hub customer stories. Each criterion is worth 1 point.

## 1. Business Objectives (1 pt)

Is there a clear customer business challenge described, driven through good discovery?

**Full point requires:**
- Specific business challenge, not generic statements
- Grounded in discovery (multi-hour sessions, not a short call)
- Uses the customer's own metrics and measurements
- No Microsoft products mentioned
- Names specific teams/functions affected

**Common deductions:**
- Too generic or obvious
- Could have come from a 15-minute call, not deep discovery
- Microsoft product names appear in this section

## 2. Hub Strategy (1 pt)

Is there a clear Hub strategy that aligns activities to the customer's business and technical challenge?

**Full point requires:**
- Clear rationale for why this engagement format was chosen for THIS customer
- Demonstrates orchestration (teams, resources, partners working together)
- Written as a recommended strategy, not an activity log
- Shows proactive, well-planned approach

**Common deductions:**
- Reads as a timeline of Hub activities (ADS → RP → follow-up)
- No explanation of why this approach vs alternatives
- Missing orchestration across teams/resources

## 3. Solutions and Outcomes (1 pt)

Is there a clear customer outcome in measured KPIs, and is the Microsoft outcome defined in revenue and/or ACR?

**Full point requires:**
- Customer KPIs with specific numbers (before/after or targets)
- Each KPI mirrors a business objective from column 1
- Microsoft outcome quantified in revenue or ACR
- If actuals unavailable, stated as agreed/expected targets

**Common deductions:**
- Generic outcomes lacking customer verification
- KPIs don't connect back to stated problems
- No Microsoft revenue impact quantified

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3/3 | Ready for submission |
| 2.5/3 | Strong — missing hard "after" metrics, note the gap |
| 2/3 | Needs work — usually missing measured outcomes or strategy framing |
| < 2 | Significant rewrite needed |

## Evaluation Workflow

1. Read the story in full to understand context
2. Score each criterion independently
3. Highlight strengths
4. Provide at least two actionable suggestions referencing the common deductions above
5. Offer a summary with score and supportive closing
